SEEKER | CAREER ROUTING ENGINE

SeekerScore.com: Conversion Audit & Action Plan

Prepared by Carlos Munoz  |  Marketing Strategy & Growth Analytics Lead  |  Seeker

Full user-journey audit conducted April 16, 2026, via live browsing session. Diagnosing why a first-time reviewer would call the product "generic" with "limited customization."

Seeker is more technically sophisticated than it appears on first look: live multi-source job matching, three-axis scoring, seniority calibration, and search transparency all exist today. None of that shows up before a visitor has already mentally filed the product as "another resume scanner." The fixes below are presentation-layer, not backend work, and require no changes to the matching or scoring logic.

The Fix: Four Objectives

ObjectiveWhat Gets DonePriority
1. Reframe the HomepageReplace scanner-category cues with market-position framing, pull the strongest differentiation line onto the homepage, fix the rendering void, remove the "0+ resumes analyzed" counter.Critical
2. Add Real Customization to UploadAdd optional target-role and target-industry inputs, show the system's detected resume attributes before analysis runs, fix the silent upload-failure error.Critical
3. Surface Intelligence in ResultsBreak the single match score into its skill / domain / seniority components, add basic filter controls, make the Insights tab functional, show the search-transparency data that already exists.High
4. Clean Up Auth & OnboardingFix the /signin 404, preview the dashboard on the sign-up page, reconsider GitHub auth for a non-technical audience.Medium

Objective 1: Reframe the Homepage

The homepage's visual and verbal vocabulary, score numbers, an "analyze your resume" CTA, an upload funnel, places Seeker in the same category as Jobscan and Resume Worded before its own differentiation gets a chance to land.

Page ElementCurrent StateWhat to Change To
Hero CTA"Analyze your resume, free" reads as standard scanner-category language."See where you'd get hired" reframes the product as market intelligence, not scanning.
Score displayStatic numbers (76% / 12 matches / 91%) mimic the exact layout of Jobscan and Resume Worded.A single sample match card with a real score breakdown, previewing the actual output format.
Differentiation placementThe strongest comparison line ("Resume scanners tell you if your font is parseable, Seeker tells you where you'd actually get hired") is buried on the Compare page, which most visitors never open.Pull that line onto the homepage, above the fold.
Social proof counter"0+ resumes analyzed" actively undercuts credibility.Remove it, or replace with a real testimonial once the count is meaningful.
Page renderingA multi-viewport black void renders between the hero and the FAQ/CTA sections (content exists in the DOM but isn't visible).Fix the underlying CSS or lazy-load issue causing the dead space.

Objective 2: Add Real Customization to Upload

The upload flow gives the user exactly two inputs, city and a remote toggle, then infers everything else silently. The inference is sophisticated; the experience of it is not.

Page ElementCurrent StateWhat to Change To
Targeting inputsOnly location and a remote-roles toggle. No target role, industry, seniority override, or salary preference.Add two optional fields: target role title and target industry. Even where the system already infers these, letting the user state them builds a sense of control.
System inference visibilitySeniority detection, domain weighting, and search expansion all happen invisibly.Show a "We detected: Senior-level, Sales/Engineering domain, Sunnyvale market" summary before analysis, with the option to correct it.
Upload error handlingFiles near the 5MB limit fail silently with a bare "Upload failed" message and no size hint (likely a client/server body-limit mismatch).Replace with a specific message: "File too large (max 4MB)" or a clear server-error message, and log the real HTTP status.

Objective 3: Surface Intelligence in Results

The results page is the most differentiated part of the product and the least visible. A reviewer who reaches it has already formed a "generic scanner" judgment from three prior pages of scanner-coded marketing.

Page ElementCurrent StateWhat to Change To
Match scoreCollapsed into one number ("89% match"), indistinguishable from a Jobscan keyword score.Show the breakdown on each match card: Skill 92% / Domain 85% / Seniority 90%.
Result controlsNo filters, no sort, no drill-down; the page is read-only.Add a minimum-score slider and a remote-only filter, minimal but enough to feel interactive.
Insights tab (demo)Non-functional; career trajectory and search transparency, the most differentiated outputs, are never seen.Make the tab functional so reviewers can preview it before uploading.
Search transparencyThe system evaluates thousands of live openings across five sources, but never says so.Add a "How we searched" line: "Evaluated 4,200 openings across Adzuna, USAJobs, Remotive, Jobicy, and Jooble." No backend change, the data already exists.

Objective 4: Clean Up Auth & Onboarding

Page ElementCurrent StateWhat to Change To
Sign-in URL/signin (no hyphen) 404s, with no redirect to the working /sign-in page.Add the redirect. Trivial fix, currently embarrassing for a product with "Sign In" in the nav.
Sign-up pagePromises "resume history" and "Pro features" with no preview of either.Show a screenshot or mockup of the dashboard on the sign-up page.
Auth optionsGitHub auth signals a developer audience, which may confuse the broader non-technical job-seeker market.Reconsider whether GitHub auth belongs here, or reserve it for a future developer-facing product surface.

Two Fixes to Ship First

FixTimeline
Fix the /signin 404 and remove the "0+ resumes analyzed" counter.Within 48 hours, both are trivial and require no backend change.
Add the skill / domain / seniority score breakdown to match cards.Within 1-2 weeks, the single highest-leverage change on this list.

What Not to Fix Right Now

Backend or matching-logic rebuild: none of the fixes above touch the aggregation, scoring, or matching engine. This is a presentation problem, not a product problem.

Full visual redesign: the dark theme itself isn't the issue, the scanner-category cues layered on top of it are. This calls for reframing, not rebranding.

Summary: The Core Diagnosis

Seeker is a career intelligence tool wrapped in the visual language and interaction model of a resume scanner. The technical differentiation is real, live multi-source matching, three-axis scoring, search transparency, but it stays server-side and invisible until deep in the funnel, if it surfaces at all.

None of the four objectives above require rebuilding what the product does. They require making visible what it already does.
